Frequently Asked Questions

What subjects does Nathan tutor?

Nathan tutors Mathematics (Pre-Algebra through Calculus III, Statistics, Discrete Math, Linear Algebra), Physics (intro through university level), Python programming, and Java programming.

If you have a related subject in mind not listed, feel free to ask — Nathan may still be able to help.

How much does tutoring cost?

Individual sessions are $45/hour for Math and Physics, and $50/hour for Python and Java. Group sessions and multi-session packages are available at discounted rates — see the Services page for full pricing.

Are sessions in-person or online?

Both options are available. Virtual sessions are held via Zoom or Google Meet and are available to students anywhere. In-person sessions are offered in the Houston, TX area at a mutually agreed-upon location (library, coffee shop, etc.).

How do I schedule a session?

Visit the Schedule page to view Nathan's availability and request a session through the embedded Google Calendar. You can also email or call Nathan directly to arrange a time.

What is the cancellation policy?

24+ hours notice: Full refund or free reschedule, no questions asked.

Less than 24 hours: The session fee is forfeited. Documented emergencies may be excepted at Nathan's discretion.

No-shows: Full session fee is charged. Please always communicate if you can't make it.

To reschedule, contact Nathan by email at nathan.a.math@gmail.com or by phone at least 24 hours in advance.

Do you offer group sessions or family packages?

Yes! Small group sessions (2–3 students) are $30/hour per student, and study groups of 4–5 students are $22/hour per student. A 10-session Family Pack covering up to 3 students is available for $400. See the Services page for all options.

What grade levels do you work with?

Nathan works with students from middle school (grades 6–8) through college level. High school students preparing for AP exams, SAT/ACT math sections, or just keeping up with coursework are all welcome.

How should I prepare for my first session?

Bring any relevant textbooks, notes, homework, or past tests you'd like to review. For programming sessions, have your development environment set up if possible (Nathan can help with setup too). Most importantly, come with specific questions or topics you want to tackle — the more focused, the more productive the session.
