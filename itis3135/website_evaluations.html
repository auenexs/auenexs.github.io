Website Evaluations

Parallel.la

  • Contrast: The site uses a dark background with light text, or vise versa when transitioning, creating strong visual contrast. The site also uses bold imagery against light text, which makes reading said text visually pleasing.
  • Repetition: The navigational links "About, Work, Contact, and Prism" appear multiple times, insisting the idea that the home page is used to navigate to these four core areas of the site.
  • Allignment: There is consistent card stucture across the entire page, with all of them being centered for visual consistency. The bodies of text are also always left alligned, creating clean reading lines.
  • Proximity: All related content is clearly grouped: Professional projects under "Our work" and newsletter content under "Our Newsletter".

Arngren.net

  • Contrast: There is no discernible typographical hierarchy. The site uses, what appears to be, the same or very similar font sizes throughout that doesn't follow any real pattern.
  • Repetition: Blue hyperlinks appear everywhere but carry no semantic meaning. Further, it seems that even the pictures are links, which defeats the purpose of the linked text. Christmas graphics also repeat extensively, which makes zero sense since the season has passed.
  • Allignment: There is absolutely zero allignment applied anywhere on the page and each individual card's content, the image and the text, can appear in any location within the card.
  • Proximity: A single product's information is often separated: image in one location, what I believe to be the product name 3 inches away, price in a third locaiton, and a link to the product page randomly somewhere else.
